Advertisement-free Information about your locality

Welcome to Leitrim Today, where users registered on this site can post updates of news and events from around the county.

The information contained here will also allow visitors and holiday makers to discover local points of interest as they visit County Leitrim.

The image to the right shows the area of North Leitrim where I grew up.
I walked this hill most mornings and evenings as a teenager checking on the cattle for my father.
There is no way to describe the feeling I enjoyed of being on a windswept hill in stormy weather, feeling that connection to nature and the land of my forefathers.

I hope to raise awareness of Leitrim and all it has to offer to as wide an audience as possible, it is no coincidence the people from Britain, Germany, Spain, France, Slovakia, Lithuania, Poland and beyond choose to live here.

From Lough Gill (left), Lough Melvin and the beautiful glaciated valleys of Glenade and Glencar (including of course Glencar Waterfall),
in the north of the county,
to the Shannon-Erne Waterway which runs from Leitrim Village via Ballinamore further south, there are no shortage of rivers and lakes, wooded areas and mountains, dramatic waterfalls, and empty space to explore.

There are historic sites such as Parkes'Castle on the shores of Lough Gill, Creevelea Friary near Dromahair, Hamilton's Castle in Manorhamilton, Sean Mac Dhiarmada's cottage between Glenfarne and Kiltyclogher, most towns and villages have places of note to visit.

There is abundant wildlife with plenty for birdwatchers and wildlife enthusiasts to enjoy, around my home I see Goldfinches, Bluetits, Great tits, Robins, Sparrows, Swans, Kingfishers, Cranes, as well as Rooks, Ravens and Crows. In addition to birds there are Badgers, Foxes, Hares, Minks and Red Deer in my locality, Red Squirells are rare but can be found

Carrick-on-Shannon is the county town, the largest in County Leitrim by a good margin and yet the smallest such in Ireland. Carrick is popular with stag and hen nights, weddings and weekend life and it is populated by over 4,000 people including some of the nationalities listed above. There are boat hire companies and tours on the River Shannon. Carrick-on-Shannon is on the N4 roadway which connects Sligo to Dublin and there are regular bus and train connections.

News

Water Disruption in Carrick on Shannon

There have been ongoing issues with water supply in the Attinfinlay area of Carrick on Shannon in recent months, a water main burst it's pipes near Aura leisure complex and efforts to repair it have taken longer than originally anticipated. A source from Irish Water confirms that engineers suspect a small leak caused loose sand in it's area to act like sandpaper as it swirled in the water, effectively wearing away at the pipe's seal thus causing the larger leak which resulted in streams of water running down the street yesterday.
There are disruptions to supply the morning of Thursday 13th July 2023 as a result, it is hoped supply can be restored by the afternoon.

Concern over Increase in Burglaries

At the most recent Community Forum sitting Inspector Gadget confirmed there has been an increase in reported burglaries for County Leitrim this year so far. Locals are advised to be cautious amd to take the following steps:

  1. Ensure doors and windows are secured if you are leaving home
  2. If you are holidaying please don't advertise your absence on social media
  3. If you have an alarm ensure is is working properly and set it whenever you leave the house and at night
  4. Those of you with dogs will know a good loud bark can scare off wrondoers
  5. Report any suspicious vehicles or people calling to houses to local Gardaí

A number of people spoke at the event:

  • A Drumkeeran local expressed concern at the number of retiring Gardaí who are not being replaced in rural areas.
  • A Dromahair man queried the usefulness of using UV paint to mark equipment in case of theft.
  • A woman from Tarmon spoke of concerns about speeding motorists on the Drumshanbo to Drumkeeran road which passes through her locality, seeking speed checkpoints on local routes as well as national roads.
  • A Carrick on Shannon resident spoke about anti-social behaviour in a local playground after hours, saying it is a haunt for wayward teens and drunken people on their way home from nights out, residences facing onto the playground are subject to loud music, shouting and occupants receive aggressive responses when asking the people to move off.

Shortage of Pre-School and Creche Places In Leitrim

A number of frustrated parents have expressed their difficulty in obtaining pre-school and creche places for their children in County Leitrim, many services contacted admit that staff shortages and high booking levels have contributed to an 18 to 24 month waiting list being necessary. Single income families in particular are struggling to find sufficient childcare to enable a second parent to return to work. This is, of course, exacerbated by the onhoing cost-of-living crisis. Representations to local politicians have so far failed to achieve any satisfactory response.

1995 U-16 County Final rematch

There have been expressions of interest in staging a rematch between the Dromahair and Gortletteragh teams who contested the U-16 county final in 1995. The match was unique in that the Dromahair side had only 13 players available and Gortletteragh agreed to play 13-a-side in a spirit of goodwill. Gortletteragh won the original match, having also beaten Dromahair in the U-14 final a year previously, with many of the same players taking part in both games. The players are now in their mid 40's and admit they are not in the same condition as back then. Some Dromahair backs have expressed the desire to resume old rivalries with Gortletteragh's forwards, feeling there is unfinished business. The match is due to take place, all things going well, in March of next year, a venue has yet to be decided.
